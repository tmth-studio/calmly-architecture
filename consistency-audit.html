Borderline conditions

Condition 1 of 2  ·  R8 Lock-in → R10 Competitive Moat

First-mover timing: EV accuracy advantage before a well-resourced competitor replicates the database

R8's switching cost and R10's competitive moat both rest on the same asset — the EV calibration database accumulated from processed claims. The asset is proprietary (Calmly-held, non-portable by ITPs) and benefits from time compression diseconomies: a competitor cannot match a five-year calibration dataset by investing more in one year.

The condition is that Calmly reaches sufficient ITP network coverage and EV accuracy before a well-resourced entrant (a litigation funder such as Burford Capital, an existing ODR platform such as Resolver, or a legal tech incumbent) deploys capital to build their own database. If such an entrant enters early — before Calmly's accuracy advantage is material to pool operator pricing — the moat has not yet formed.

Critical assumption to test: At what claims volume does Calmly's EV accuracy become materially superior to a new entrant's starting accuracy — and does the implementation roadmap reach that volume before likely competitor entry? The implementation sequencing must explicitly target the ITP network activation timeline and the claims volume milestone required to establish a defensible accuracy gap.
Condition 2 of 2  ·  R2 Efficacy → R7 Defendant Notification

Defendant notification template must carry all three R2/C6 signals reliably through the ITP channel

R2's efficacy mechanism requires two signals to reach the defendant simultaneously at D1: (1) an independent EV assessment the defendant cannot dismiss as self-interested; (2) an escalation schedule showing the rising cost of delay at each litigation stage. Both together disrupt the defendant's motivated reasoning and make early settlement the rational response.

C6 adds a third element to the D1 notification package: (3) a conditional liability buyout offer from a litigation finance firm — a fixed-price, time-limited offer for legal closure before the defendant has agreed to settle. The notification package is now richer, but the ITP editing risk is correspondingly higher.

R7 routes the entire notification package through the ITP's institutional channel. The ITP "reviews and forwards" a pre-drafted template. Two distinct editing risks now apply:

  • Risk 1 — escalation schedule softening: HR departments may remove or soften the cost-of-delay language to protect their institutional relationship with the defendant (often an employee). If removed, signal (2) is lost and R2's urgency framing is degraded.
  • Risk 2 — buyout offer removal: The buyout offer is a commercial instrument from a named third-party firm. HR departments and housing associations may strip it before forwarding — it looks commercially adversarial in a way the EV number and escalation schedule do not. If removed, C6's proactive legal closure mechanism is inaccessible to the defendant at D1; the buyout either never fires or fires later through a separate channel, losing its architectural advantage.

If the package is delivered intact, both risks resolve into advantages: the escalation schedule and the buyout offer together eliminate the "stalling is costless" strategy — the defendant faces a time-limited window in which legal closure is available at a known price. The urgency framing is stronger than the two-signal package alone.

Critical assumptions to test (two, not one): (1) Will ITPs forward the escalation schedule language without softening? Test with HR and housing association contacts. Frame template language as operational (providing accurate information about next steps), not adversarial. (2) Will ITPs forward a conditional buyout offer from a named litigation finance firm through their institutional channel? This requires the ITP to understand that the offer benefits their member (the defendant) — the ITP is facilitating a resolution option for their own community member, not acting as a debt collector. Field-test both elements before launch. Resolve in R7 product design, not in R2 or C6.
