The marketplace infrastructure (pooling, settlement mechanism, payment rails) is Calmly's. Calmly does not own claims, bear litigation risk, or employ solicitors — those belong to the pool operator.
Governing statement

This CTM maps the state changes required for claimants and defendants in England and Wales with unresolved small claims (£500–£5,000) to reach a settlement through Calmly's C1+C2+C3 architecture — a pooling mechanism that makes litigation credible at portfolio level, combined with a two-sided value mechanism that removes each party's key monetizable cost, combined with an independent assessment organisation that forecloses source-credibility delay tactics in mediation (C3).

Both claimants and defendants are paying customers. Both journeys must reach the shared HIO: "we reach a settlement that reflects the merits of the case." C1 makes that settlement structurally possible. C2 makes it happen early and at fair value. Every state change below follows from one of those two mechanisms.

Two-customer architecture

C2 names two distinct customers, each with a distinct key monetizable cost. Both receive value. Both pay. The CTM must therefore map two parallel journeys that converge at a single point — settlement — which is the shared HIO.

Current state — before C1+C2

Structurally stranded — both parties

  • Claimant: Has a legitimate claim. Cannot credibly threaten litigation — no solicitor will take the case, costs are unrecoverable. Defendant correctly reads the threat as empty. Non-participation is rational.
  • Claimant KMC: The settlement amount already owed is framed as a loss being actively sustained — not a potential future gain. Defendant's delay strategy exploits temporal discounting.
  • Defendant: Faces a complaint but correctly assesses that pursuit is irrational for an individual claimant. Stonewalls. Does not know — or wants not to know — what they legitimately owe.
  • Defendant KMC: Cannot distinguish a fair claim from an inflated one. Every valuation is adversarial. Open-ended uncertainty drives either over-settlement or motivated disengagement. Both are more expensive than a fair settlement.
  • Dispute resolves through abandonment or inadequate capitulation — not merit.
Transformed state — after C1+C2

Settlement at fair value — rational for both

  • Claimant: Claim is pooled. The pool pursues at portfolio level — making litigation the rational committed strategy for the pool operator. Individual variance is irrelevant. Claimant is no longer the threatening party.
  • Claimant C2 transform: Delay is reframed as active loss accrual. The settlement already owed is a loss being sustained each week. Early resolution is the rational response — not capitulation, but recovery.
  • Defendant: Faces a credible adversary (C1) and receives an independent EV assessment from a party with no financial stake in the outcome (C2). The signal is credible by design. Motivated disengagement loses its factual basis.
  • Defendant C2 transform: Open-ended uncertainty collapses to a bounded, calculable liability. The defendant can now act rationally rather than stonewalling. Settlement at fair value becomes the dominant response.
  • Shared HIO: Settlement reflects the merits of the case — because both parties now have the information and the incentive structure to make it rational.

Business Form Factor — C1+C2: A mediator that helps claimants pool their claims, pursue them on shared costs and revenues, and provides both parties with an independent assessment of the claim's expected value — giving the claimant a factual basis for what they are owed and the defendant a credible signal they cannot rationally dismiss.

Platform note: Calmly facilitates this. The pool operator holds the claims and bears the litigation risk. Calmly earns platform fees from both parties per claim resolved.

Claimant journey

From "I have an unresolved claim" → pooling → EV assessment → settlement

Phase 1 — Becoming a customer (claimant)

Start state: claimant has an unresolved dispute, no workable path, and correctly believes the defendant has no reason to engage. End state: claimant has joined the pool, understands their claim will be pursued, has an independent reference point for its value, and knows their contribution is insured against early losses. Note: awareness and channel (how the claimant finds Calmly) are C7 mechanisms. The C1–C4 architecture provides the mechanism; elements requiring C5+ are flagged [REQUIRES C5+] where applicable.

1A

Awareness

Unaware → aware that pooling changes their position
C1 premise Claimant — 1A Awareness
Cognitive — must think
"There is a way to pursue my claim that doesn't require me to fund it alone. When claims are pooled, someone will actually follow through — and the other side knows it."
Emotive — must feel
Surprised — most claimants have already concluded their only options are expensive or hopeless. Cautious — not yet convinced the pool applies to their case. The emotional shift is from resigned to cautiously interested.
Behavioural — must do
Encounters a description of the pool concept. Reads enough to recognise that the credibility problem has a structural solution. Does not yet need to understand how the pool is managed or how they would be paid.

[C7 — ITP referral mechanism] The claimant encounters Calmly through their ITP (Intermediating Third Party) — the mutual dispute resolution authority already present in their ecosystem. When a dispute arises (housing association complaint, HR grievance, marketplace dispute), the ITP's standard escalation process now includes Calmly as the default pathway for qualifying claims (value ≥ threshold, recoverable without court costs). The ITP provides: (1) one-page explainer at first contact, (2) digital referral form (<5 min to complete), (3) automatic outcome notification to close the loop. State change this enables: claimant moves from unaware to aware that a resolution pathway exists within their existing ecosystem — without needing to seek external legal advice.

4-D product component
Communications Product — pool concept statement. The logical premise that individual pursuit is irrational and pooled pursuit is credible. No channel specified at C1+C2 scope.
1B

Consideration

Aware → beginning to believe pooling changes their specific situation
C1 premise Claimant — 1B Consideration
Cognitive — must think
"The reason I couldn't get anywhere is that I was pursuing this alone. The defendant knew I wouldn't follow through. If the pool pursues — not me — the defendant faces a different adversary."
Emotive — must feel
Intellectually engaged — working through a new frame. The C1 premise is not emotionally obvious: "the pool threatens so I don't have to" is an abstract concept. The emotional shift — from hopeless to plausibly hopeful — depends on whether the logical premise lands. Doubt persists: "but what's in it for whoever runs the pool?"
Behavioural — must do
Reads or asks enough to understand pool economics — why the pool pursues, not just that it does. Begins comparing the pool path against self-directed pursuit or abandonment. The main residual doubt at this stage: "Will the pool survive long enough financially? Early claims may lose — what happens to my contribution if the pool's cash is drawn down before the expected value materialises?"
[C4 mechanism] Pool financial survivability doubt is resolved by pool loss insurance — a standard feature of pool membership (not an add-on). Individual claimant contributions are protected against early losses. No individual faces the financial incentive to exit early because their downside is insured. The pool persists through the natural loss distribution. Olson (1965): collective action failures under sequential losses are resolved when individual exposure is transferred to a risk-pooling mechanism. Arrow (1963): insurance resolves the individual incentive to defect by converting idiosyncratic downside risk into a shared actuarial pool.
4-D product component
Communications Product — pool economics explanation. The Schelling precommitment argument: at portfolio level, the pool operator's payoff makes litigating every qualifying claim the dominant strategy. The claimant does not need to fund it. The pool does.
1C

Conviction

Considering → convinced the pool will pursue and their claim has a factual basis for value
C1 mechanism C2a — EV assessment Claimant — 1C Conviction
Cognitive — must think
"The pool operator has a payoff structure that makes pursuit rational — that is what makes the threat credible. And I have an independent reference point for what my claim is worth: the cost of legal escalation is the value I recover by resolving this early rather than waiting for the defendant to force court."
Emotive — must feel
Trust in the mechanism — not relational trust in Calmly, but structural trust in the pool's payoff logic. Additionally, at C2 level: relief that the cost of delay is now visible and calculable. The open-ended stress of waiting collapses to a specific, bounded number — the legal escalation cost being actively accrued.
Behavioural — must do
Accepts the pool logic. Understands the cost of the defendant's delay as active loss accrual — every week of non-settlement increases the legal escalation costs that could have been avoided. Moves toward sign-up.
[REQUIRES C3+] A claim-specific independent valuation — confirming this particular claim is worth pursuing — requires the pre-sale indicative valuation from the rating organisation. That is a C5 mechanism. At C1+C2 scope, conviction rests on the pool concept and the C2a cost framing. Claim-specific qualification is not yet available.
4-D product component
Working Product — pool pursuit mechanism (C1). Communications Product — C2a loss framing: the settlement already owed is a loss being actively sustained. The cost of legal escalation — any work to progress through the legal system that could have been avoided by earlier settlement — is reframed as the claimant's key monetizable cost. Prospect Theory (Kahneman & Tversky 1979): losses loom larger than equivalent gains.
1D

Sign-up

Convinced → joined the pool, claim submitted
C1 mechanism C4 — insurance Claimant — 1D Sign-up
Cognitive — must think
"I am pooling my claim with others. Costs and revenues are shared. My contribution is insured — if early claims lose, my upfront input is protected. The pool will persist long enough for the expected value to materialise. I am not doing this alone, and I am not exposed to early variance."
Emotive — must feel
Relief at having a path and structural confidence that the path will hold. Insurance removes the residual anxiety that "even if the pool concept works, it might fall apart before my claim is resolved." The budget constraint is removed in principle (no upfront payment); the contribution is also protected in practice (insurance). Residual uncertainty about the specific payment amount — the exact share structure is a C6 design question.
Behavioural — must do
Submits claim and evidence. Agrees to pool participation terms (costs and revenues shared across the pool, contributions insured as standard). Does not pay anything upfront.
[C4 mechanism] Pool membership includes pool loss insurance as a standard feature — not an add-on. The insurance app is a verified app on the Calmly marketplace, curated by Calmly and underwritten by a specialist legal expenses insurer. The claimant's contribution is protected from the moment of joining.
[REQUIRES C5+] The onboarding flow, evidence submission process, and specific terms of the payment instrument are product design questions not yet fully specified. The principle (payment contingent on recovery from the pool) is the C6 amortisation strategy — but the detailed mechanism is a C6 design output. Pre-sale indicative valuation (claim-specific EV before commitment) is C5.
4-D product component
Working Product — pool participation mechanism (C1). Communications Product — pool loss insurance (C4): pool membership includes insurance as standard, protecting individual contributions regardless of early claim outcomes. Payment Product — contingency structure in principle: payment from recovered money, not existing funds. No upfront cost.

Phase 2 — Consuming the product (claimant)

Start state: claim is in the pool. The claimant is no longer the threatening party — the pool is. End state: the pool's threat reaches the defendant, mediation is operative, and the defendant's rational calculus has changed. The claimant's Phase 2 is largely passive — the pool acts on their behalf. Value arrives when the defendant engages.

2A

Activation

Claim in pool → operational burden transfers to the pool
C1 mechanism Claimant — 2A Activation
Cognitive — must think
"My claim is in the pool. The pool will pursue it. I do not need to do anything else."
Emotive — must feel
Passive expectation. The emotional shift from active stress (self-directing a failing dispute) to passive waiting (pool operating on their behalf) is significant. The claimant no longer holds the operational burden. The C2a framing — delay as active loss — means they feel time working in their favour rather than against them.
Behavioural — must do
No required action. Waits for the pool to initiate contact with the defendant.

[C7 — ITP defendant notification] When the claimant submits to the pool, the ITP receives a pre-drafted defendant notification template. The ITP reviews and forwards it to the defendant through the ITP's existing communication channel — housing association correspondence, HR system, or marketplace messaging. The defendant receives a formal demand from the authority both parties already recognise — not from Calmly directly. This is the two-sided function of the ITP: it is the mutual authority within whose ecosystem the dispute arose. Notification through the ITP carries institutional weight neither party can dismiss.

4-D product component
Working Product — pool operation. The pool acts on the claimant's behalf. This is the core structural shift C1 produces: the threatening party is now the pool operator, not the claimant.
2B

Engagement — mediation

Pool threat active → defendant engages, claimant participates
C1 mechanism C2a — loss framing operative Claimant — 2B Engagement
Cognitive — must think
"The defendant is engaging. The pool threat worked. The legal escalation cost the defendant is accruing each week makes early settlement the rational move for them too."
Emotive — must feel
Vindication — the first external confirmation that the grievance is legitimate and the adversary had to engage. This moment carries disproportionate emotional weight. At C2 level: the time pressure is now on the defendant, not the claimant. The power asymmetry has inverted.
Behavioural — must do
Participates in mediation — likely passively, through the pool's mediation process rather than direct claimant-defendant negotiation.
[REQUIRES C3+] The mediation process itself — who runs it, what the defendant is presented with, how a settlement figure is proposed, who is present — is not designed at C1+C2 scope. C1 establishes mediation is operative; C2 establishes the time-cost dynamic. The operating model is a product design question.
4-D product component
Working Product — mediation facilitation. C1 makes engagement operative. C2a applies: with each week of delay, the defendant's legal escalation costs increase — a manufactured "savings" the defendant loses by waiting, creating genuine time pressure.

Phase 3 — Payment (claimant)

C1 establishes that costs and revenues are shared across the pool. C6 (amortisation) confirms no upfront cost is required — payment is contingent on recovery. The budget line problem dissolves structurally: payment and value arrive at the same moment.

3A

Payment

Settlement reached → claimant receives share of pool recovery
C1 premise C2a — loss resolved Claimant — 3A Payment
Cognitive — must think
"I shared the costs of pursuing this and I receive a share of what was recovered. I paid nothing upfront — my contribution was the claim itself. The legal escalation costs the defendant avoided through early settlement reduced what they had to pay."
Emotive — must feel
Closure. The open loop closes. The loss being actively sustained — the C2a KMC — is resolved. The emotional anchor is not "I won in court" but "the thing that was hanging over me is done." C2a framing means the claimant's sense of justice is about recovery, not punishment.
Behavioural — must do
Receives payment from the pool's recovery. No action required — the pool distributes.
[REQUIRES C3+] The payment structure — contingent on outcome, embedded in the pool's revenue-sharing model, delivered through the ATE mutual or equivalent instrument — is a C6 (amortisation strategy) design problem. C1 says costs and revenues are shared. C6 specifies how. The payment amount, timing, and instrument are not defined at C1+C2 scope.
4-D product component
Payment Product — contingent recovery structure. Payment contingent on settlement or judgment outcome; claimant pays from recovered money, not existing funds. No budget line required.

Phase 4 — Repeating (claimant)

Retention at C1+C2 is structurally thin. The pool concept and the C2a cost resolution — once experienced as effective — provide a rational basis for return if a future dispute arises. Structured lock-in (C8) is not yet in scope.

4A

Retention

Outcome delivered → claimant holds a rational basis for returning
C1 premise Claimant — 4A Retention
Cognitive — must think
"Pooling worked. If I have another dispute, this is the path — not courts, not lawyers, not abandonment."
Emotive — must feel
Residual trust in the mechanism — structural trust (the payoff logic holds) persists without relationship maintenance. Individual claimants are low-frequency and event-driven; the more important retention dynamic is at the ITP partner level, not the individual level.
Behavioural — must do
No active re-engagement required. Returns if a new dispute arises.
[REQUIRES C5+] Structured lock-in — case history reusable, accumulated credentials reducing future onboarding friction, ITP recommending return — is a C8 mechanism. C1+C2 provides the rational premise for return. Switching cost creation is not yet designed.
4-D product component
Communications Product — outcome-driven return prompt. The pool concept, once experienced as effective, is sufficient rational basis for future return at C1+C2 scope.

Defendant journey

From "I have received a claim" → independent EV signal → settlement decision

The defendant is a paying customer. Their key monetizable cost is the cost of not knowing what they legitimately owe. C2b resolves this through Spence signalling — an independent EV assessment from a party with no financial stake. C1 is the precondition: it changes the defendant's rational calculus so that engagement is necessary. C2b makes settlement at fair value the rational response.

Phase 1 — Awareness and engagement (defendant)

The defendant does not voluntarily seek Calmly. They receive a notification. Their journey begins at the point of contact — and their first state change is the recognition that the adversary they face is not the individual claimant they could previously ignore.

D1A

Receipt of demand

Unaware → receives pool demand, assesses position
C1 mechanism — core Defendant — D1A Receipt
Cognitive — must think
"I have received a demand from an entity that has pooled multiple claims and will pursue them. This is not an individual claimant I can wait out. The party now threatening me does not have the same payoff problem I have always relied on."
Emotive — must feel
Unease — the defendant's standard calculation (the claimant will give up) no longer holds. The pool operator is not emotionally invested in this specific case. They are making an EV decision across a portfolio. That is a qualitatively different adversary. Cluster 1 (SME/corporate): institutional recognition of a changed threat environment. Cluster 2 (micro/sole trader): personal unease, suppressed guilt surfacing.
Behavioural — must do
Receives the pool's demand. Assesses their position. For the first time, non-engagement carries a genuine consequence: court, pursued by a party whose payoff structure makes following through the dominant strategy.

[C3 UPDATE] The defendant's capacity to extend mediation on source-credibility grounds is foreclosed by the independent assessment org (C3). The report is generated by law students with no financial stake — the objection "Calmly has an interest in the settlement amount" loses its structural basis. Without this objection, the primary delay tactic within mediation is unavailable.

4-D product component
Working Product — pool credibility mechanism. The pool's payoff structure makes litigating every qualifying claim the dominant strategy (Schelling 1960). Non-participation is no longer rational. Mediation becomes operative.
D1B

Independent EV signal received

Threat received → defendant receives credible signal of what they legitimately owe
C2b — Spence signalling C3 Defendant — D1B EV signal
Cognitive — must think
"I have received an independent assessment of what I legitimately owe from a party that has no financial stake in whether I settle or not. This is not the claimant's valuation. It is not Calmly's valuation. It is a signal I cannot dismiss on the grounds that it is motivated."
Emotive — must feel
The open-ended uncertainty — "how much do I actually owe?" — collapses to a bounded, calculable liability. The emotional relief of knowing the number is real, even if the number is uncomfortable. For Cluster 2 (sole trader): the guilt that was suppressed through motivated reasoning now has a specific addressable target. Acknowledgment becomes possible.
Behavioural — must do
Evaluates the independent EV assessment. Compares it against their own self-serving estimate. The gap between the two is now publicly visible to both parties — which changes the settlement negotiation dynamics.
4-D product component
Working Product — independent EV assessment. Spence, M. (1973): a signal from a party with no financial stake in the outcome is credible by design. The information asymmetry that made motivated disengagement rational collapses. Defendant can now act rationally: settle at fair value rather than over-settle or stonewall. C3 update: assessment generated by independent assessment org — law students, no financial stake. Spence (1973): signal credible because structural conditions for motivated bias are absent.

Phase 2 — Consuming the product (defendant)

The defendant's Phase 2 is active, not passive. They are making a rational settlement calculation in real time. C1 established that non-engagement leads to court. C2b provides the information needed to make the settlement decision rational rather than resistant.

D2A

Mediation engagement

EV signal received → defendant engages in mediation
C1 mechanism C2b — bounded liability Defendant — D2A Mediation engagement
Cognitive — must think
"My best outcome now is a settlement that reflects the merits — not a wait-and-see. Refusal leads to court with a credible adversary. Delay adds to the cost I will eventually pay. The number I am being asked to settle at is independently assessed; I cannot dismiss it as motivated."
Emotive — must feel
A shift from passive stonewalling to active rational engagement. Not necessarily willing — but rational. The defendant participates because the alternative is court with a credible adversary (C1) and the settlement amount is bounded and independently justified (C2b). The without-prejudice framing removes the liability cascade fear: any settlement offer cannot be used as evidence of admission in other proceedings.
Behavioural — must do
Engages in mediation. Responds to the pool's demand. Makes a settlement offer or enters negotiation from a position where the expected value is known to both parties.
4-D product component
Working Product — mediation platform. C1 makes engagement necessary (BATNA has changed). C2b makes engagement rational (bounded liability rather than open-ended exposure). Without-prejudice framework is the enabling condition for genuine engagement: the door opened by engaging stays closed beyond this claim.
D2B

Early settlement

Engaged → settles early to avoid accruing legal escalation costs
C2b — loss accrual (Prospect Theory) Defendant — D2B Early settlement
Cognitive — must think
"Each week I delay, the cost of resolution increases. The savings I could have made by settling last week are now gone. Early settlement is not a concession — it is the rational move given the time structure of the costs I face."
Emotive — must feel
Urgency — manufactured by the cost-of-delay mechanism. This is Prospect Theory applied to the defendant's frame: delay is reframed from a neutral hold (no immediate cost) to active loss accrual (costs increasing with each week of non-settlement). The emotional register shifts from passive resistance to calculated urgency.
Behavioural — must do
Makes a settlement offer at or near the independently assessed fair value. Does not wait. The "savings" manufactured by C2 — the legal escalation costs the defendant would lose by waiting — make earlier settlement the dominant strategy.

C2 Efficacy Strategy (Defendant): Manufacture a store of "savings" for the defendant that they will lose if they delay settlement, akin to a switching cost. With each week of non-settlement, the defendant's legal escalation costs increase. Framed as loss accrual (Prospect Theory), not future expense, this changes the temporal discounting calculation decisively.

4-D product component
Working Product — cost-of-delay schedule. Kahneman & Tversky (1979): losses loom approximately twice as large as equivalent gains. The time-indexed cost-of-delay mechanism reframes each week of non-settlement as a loss the defendant is actively incurring — not a future discretionary payment. Early settlement becomes the rational response.

Phase 3 — Payment (defendant)

The defendant's payment is the settlement itself. Their key monetizable cost — the cost of not knowing what they legitimately owe — has been eliminated by C2b. Payment at fair value is now the dominant rational strategy.

D3A

Settlement payment

Agreement reached → defendant pays fair settlement
C2b resolved Defendant — D3A Settlement payment
Cognitive — must think
"I am paying what I legitimately owe — no more. The independent assessment gave me the factual basis to know that. I am not over-settling out of fear, and I am not under-settling out of motivated reasoning."
Emotive — must feel
Resolution without admission cascade. The without-prejudice framing means paying does not constitute an admission for other proceedings. For Cluster 2 defendants (sole traders): optional acknowledgment pathway — a structured non-admissible statement — allows genuine psychological closure rather than purely financial settlement. Tangney (1994): guilt resolves through acknowledgment and amends, not through financial settlement alone.
Behavioural — must do
Pays the settled amount. Closes the dispute. No further legal proceedings.
4-D product component
Payment Product — settlement execution. Without-prejudice framing as enabling condition. Optional acknowledgment pathway for Cluster 2 defendants (not required for payment; available for psychological closure).
Convergence — shared HIO

Both journeys meet at settlement

The claimant journey and the defendant journey converge at a single point: settlement that reflects the merits of the case. This is the shared HIO — the job both parties hired Calmly to do.

C1 makes the convergence structurally possible: without the pool, the defendant has no rational reason to engage, and the claimant has no path to resolution. C2 makes convergence happen early and at fair value: C2a manufactures time pressure on the defendant through loss framing; C2b gives both parties a common independent reference point for what fair value is.

Neither mechanism alone is sufficient for the HIO. C1 without C2: the defendant engages but may delay indefinitely and dispute the value. C2 without C1: the defendant has an independent signal but no credible threat forcing them to act on it. Together, they produce a settlement that reflects the merits — which is the definition of the HIO.

Review findings

Logical viability State transitions follow directly from the mechanisms — no non-sequiturs

Every state change in the CTM follows from one of two mechanisms: the pool's payoff structure (C1) or the EV/loss framing (C2). The transitions are logically ordered — a claimant who has not reached conviction (1C) cannot meaningfully enter the pool (1D). A defendant who has not received the EV signal (D1B) cannot engage rationally in mediation (D2A). Each sub-phase is a genuine prerequisite for the next.

One tension worth naming: the claimant's conviction at 1C rests partly on the C2a loss framing — but the full C2a mechanism (time-indexed cost of delay applied to the defendant) requires the defendant to already be in the process. This is not a logical failure — the claimant's conviction can rest on the pool concept alone at C1, and C2a supplements it. But the C2a framing at conviction is prospective ("this is how it will work") rather than demonstrated ("here is evidence it worked"). The C4 and C5 mechanisms will close this gap.

Elegance C2b operates on both customers simultaneously — one signal, two transformations

The independent EV assessment (C2b) is an elegant double-function mechanism. For the defendant, it collapses open-ended uncertainty to a bounded liability — the Spence signal. For the claimant, the same assessment gives them a factual basis for what they are owed — a symmetric information benefit. One product component drives two distinct state changes across two customer journeys. This is worth preserving explicitly in the AOM: the EV assessment service is a shared cost that produces value on both sides of the platform.

Similarly, the without-prejudice framing operates on both sides: it removes the defendant's legal objection to engaging (liability cascade risk) while providing the claimant with a more genuine settlement rather than a technical minimum-to-avoid-admission offer.

Realistic progressions Phase 1 for the claimant is logically coherent but practically incomplete without C5+ (origination, claim qualification, payment instrument)

C4 closes the pool financial survivability doubt at 1B and 1D. Every Phase 1 sub-phase now has a mechanism — but several conversion-critical mechanisms are still pending:

  • 1A (awareness) origination channel is now resolved: ITP (Intermediating Third Party) routes the claimant at dispute onset via the referral protocol (one-page explainer + digital form, <5 min). Housing associations, HR departments, and online marketplaces are the three ITP types. See 1A mechanism above.
  • 1B (consideration) — pool financial survivability doubt is now resolved by C4 insurance. C4 complete.
  • 1C (conviction) is strengthened by claim-specific qualification — which requires the pre-sale indicative valuation (C5). Without it, conviction rests on the pool concept and C2a loss framing alone.
  • 1D (sign-up) — pool membership with insurance is now specified (C4). The specific payment instrument and distribution mechanism remain C6 detail.

The architecture is becoming operationally complete at Phase 1. The remaining gaps (origination channel, claim-specific EV before commitment, payment instrument) are sequencing problems, not architecture failures.

Strategic operationalisation C2b does more work than the standard practice it overrides

The standard industry approach to defendant engagement is coercive: serve notice, threaten court, hope the defendant folds. C2b does something structurally different — it provides the defendant with the information they need to act rationally. This is the Spence insight: the signal changes behaviour not through threat but through information provision. The AOM implication is significant: the EV assessment operation is not a legal operation (threatening notice), it is an analytical operation (independent valuation). The staffing profile and cost structure are different — and considerably cheaper.

The AOM should treat the EV assessment as a platform service, not a legal service. Calmly operates the exchange. The pool operator runs the litigation. The EV assessment is the market infrastructure that makes rational behaviour possible on both sides.
