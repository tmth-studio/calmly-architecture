Should I pursue the venture?

Question addressed: given my life goals, should I pursue Calmly?
Answer
Yes. Pursue Calmly — because the life strategy requires it, the numbers independently justify it, and the work is the awakening.
  1. The life strategy already committed Tom to this — the question is whether to keep the commitment, not whether to make it.
  2. The numbers independently justify continuing — FMOS clears comfortably, EV is overwhelmingly positive, and Phase I costs almost nothing.
  3. The design work deepens awakening — it is the practice, not a distraction from it.

In April 2026, a life strategy was built from the apex down. The apex is awakening through right livelihood — work aligned with values, well-paid enough, serving, not grasping. Three layers down, Calmly is Bet 2: ship to FMOS ≥ 60% by end 2027. R1–R4 are complete. The architecture is documented in Lucid.

The question still deserves honest treatment. The job search is Q2's first priority. The venture has been in design for several years without reaching market. The pilot was deferred from Q2 to Q3. It would be easy to continue by default — carrying the commitment without ever confirming it is still the right one.

It is. Three reasons follow.

The life strategy already made the commitment — the question is whether to keep it

Calmly is not adjacent to the life strategy. It is load-bearing within it.

The pyramid traces from the mission apex — awakening through equipping people the system cannot see to build new structures — through a ten-year institutional vision, to three-year Bet 2: ship Calmly to FMOS ≥ 60%. The bet is explicit, named, and dated. Walking away would not be a pivot. It would break the architecture that every other bet sits inside.

The specific load-bearing point: Simanis has staked his reputation on Calmly. Built to Hold — his canonical paper, the primary manuscript for the IVE methodology — takes Calmly as its worked case. The venture-assistant (Bet 3) is the tool for transmitting the IVE methodology at scale to 100 founders by 2029. If the headline worked example fails, the tool transmits a methodology whose proof point collapsed. Bet 3 depends on Bet 2 working.

What this means in practice: Tom is co-author on the Cambridge / YNOT Institute working papers that explicitly attribute Calmly. The institutional credibility — the credential stack that makes a teaching institution possible in 2036 — runs through this venture working. It is not optional.

There is a more personal commitment sitting beneath the strategic one. Tom told Erik that Calmly would go live. Pursuing it now is fulfilling that agreement. This is not about doing Erik a favour — the MCL sessions have ended, and the relationship has its complications. It is about something simpler: Tom keeps his agreements for his own sake, not for anyone else's. The integrity belongs to Tom, not to the recipient.

The question "should I pursue this?" is asked inside a structure that already answered it. The real question is simpler: will the commitments made — to the life strategy, and to Erik — be kept?

The numbers independently justify it — Phase I costs almost nothing against an overwhelming expected return

The financial case stands on its own, separate from the strategic argument.

Phase I — the design work, the architecture development, the ongoing refinement — costs almost nothing. Tom's time is the only input. No capital is at risk.

The gross spread between Calmly's purchase price and average case settlement is £670 per case. After capital charges, the net spread before operating costs is approximately £605. The FMOS gate — Simanis's financial margin of safety required before committing Phase II capital — clears up to £367 of operating cost per case. Given the tiered student structure (second-year law students unpaid, trainee solicitors unpaid as supervisors, third-years at minimum wage), realistic operating costs sit well below that threshold. The gate clears comfortably across nearly every plausible operating cost scenario.

Expected value of Phase I: ~£3.6M against a cost of ~£15k. The ratio is 240 to 1. For Phase I to have negative expected value, the probability of ultimate success would need to fall below 0.1% — practically certain failure. That is not a credible read of the design position at R1–R4 complete.

At IVE-convention at-scale — 3–5% of the 8.7M TAM, giving 261,000–435,000 cases per year — FCF is estimated at £80–200M per year (central: ~£105–163M, depending on which FMOS denominator is confirmed). The March 2025 simulation's full-scale figure of 655,000 cases per year exceeds this range: it represents 7.5% TAM penetration, above the IVE 5-year target convention. The financial case does not require optimistic inputs to clear the bar — it clears it on the conservative sub-TAM estimate as well.

The TAM is 8.7M cases, drawn from legal needs surveys — the Ipsos survey commissioned by the Law Society and Legal Services Board covers the full population of people experiencing a legal problem, including those who take no action. This is not the 1.5M who file in court each year. It is the much larger pool of people who have a dispute and currently do nothing, because the cost of action is too high. Calmly's model is designed to reach that latent demand, not just the fraction who have already decided to litigate.

What this means for the family. Tom currently holds 50% of the venture. A Phase II equity raise of £1–3M would dilute this to approximately 38–42%. Working capital at scale — the larger capital requirement — is best funded by a litigation finance facility rather than equity, and should not dilute the stake further.

At central scale by the time Tom turns 40 (23% of full capacity, 40% equity post-dilution), the exit value is approximately £189M net of capital gains tax, or £25M a year in dividends. The bear case — 12% of scale — produces £94M net on sale, or £12M a year. The house, financial independence for Pauline, the institutional endowment: all of it sits inside the bear case with room to spare.

The six assumptions behind the £189M figure are set out in the appendix, with confidence ratings and sensitivity analysis for each.

Pursuing Calmly deepens awakening — the design work is the practice itself

The Q2 apex is "to deepen my own awakening." The filter for every decision: does this serve awakening? The question deserves a rigorous answer — not an assertion. Four mechanisms connect the venture work to the practice. They are distinct.

The first is karma yoga — action without attachment to outcome. The Bhagavad Gita's central teaching is to act fully while holding the result lightly. Calmly gives this a live test rather than an abstract one. The central-case exit is £189M. Non-attachment is not philosophical here. Can Tom do the work with full commitment while not grasping at the return? Every design session repeats the question. The life strategy names Calmly as a means, not an end. If it deepens awakening and produces nothing financially, it has done its job. If that is genuinely true, the work is practice.

The second is integrity under difficulty. The relationship with Simanis has frayed. The reactive self wants to renegotiate the commitment — to make the decision about Erik rather than about Tom. The response: keep the agreement for Tom's own sake, independent of the recipient and independent of how the relationship has gone. This is not incidental to the venture — it is the practice. Acting from values rather than from reaction produces the same result that formal sitting trains: contact with what is, rather than with what you want it to be.

The third is right livelihood as path, not as context. The Buddha's Eightfold Path includes right livelihood as one of the eight limbs — not as background, as practice. Calmly is explicitly designed as right livelihood: aligned with values, well-paid enough, serving, not grasping, not ascetic. In this framing, there is no "and then I meditate." The design work is the meditation.

The fourth is the quality of attention the work demands. The IVE design loop — diagnosing the Critical Limiting Operation, deducing the structural workaround, productising the solution, simulating the economics — requires a kind of attention structurally similar to koan work. It cannot be solved by force. It requires sitting with not-knowing, following the logic where it leads rather than where you want it to go, holding the whole system without collapsing into any single part. This is the same attentional faculty that sustained formal practice trains — not as metaphor, but as the same capacity exercised in a different container.

The honest condition. The argument holds only if Tom meets the work with presence rather than contraction. Grinding through it — contracted, grasping at the exit, carrying unexamined bitterness — is effort, not practice. Shinzen Young's point: difficulty met with presence opens. Difficulty met with resistance closes. The venture provides the difficulty. Tom provides the quality of attention. When the going gets hard and doubt arrives, the question to return to is not "is this deepening my awakening?" — that makes awakening into an object to evaluate. The question is simpler: am I here, or am I contracted? If Tom is present with what is — the difficulty, the uncertainty, the relationship that has not gone as hoped — the deepening is already happening.

Tom wants to do it. That, in the end, is the warrant. Chandler's view: genuine wanting is sufficient condition. Not because wanting overrides everything else, but because wanting that survives years of design work, a deferred pilot, and a relationship that has had its difficulties — that is the signal that the practice is real.
