Section 1 — F: Financial margin of safety (FMOS)

⚠ Reconciliation note — AOM v1 vs fin-sim v3 cost discrepancy

The previous FIT Verifier gate (18 May 2026) returned BORDERLINE with one outstanding condition: reconcile the AOM v1 cost floor (£480/claim) with the fin-sim v3 cost floor (£315/claim). Both used the same law clinic module architecture (solicitors + students) but different methodologies (bottom-up hourly vs top-down annual budget).

This gate uses the AOM v2 cost structure — the exchange model. AOM v2 excludes solicitor and student costs from Calmly's P&L (pool operators bear these) and replaces them with lower-cost LMU specialist roles. This is architecturally correct for the v2 exchange model. The cost differential (£307 fin-sim vs £236 AOM v2) is explained by the £71/claim solicitor cost that moves to the pool operator. Fin-sim v3 needs to be rebuilt for v2 before a capital decision is made — flagged as a condition below.

Section 2 — I: Intrinsic performance capability
Pass with flag

The exchange model has a structurally embedded cost reduction mechanism: LMU specialist roles replace high-cost solicitor time for all activities within Calmly's P&L. As volume scales, HQ overhead and platform amortisation dilute per-claim, producing an intrinsic performance curve. The per-claim cost of the exchange infrastructure falls as claim volume rises — this is a design property, not an operational achievement.

Flag (management risk): The CP3 defendant engagement activity (86 min per confirmed claim, the largest single labour cost) depends on skilled negotiators to achieve pre-trial settlement. If settlement rates fall below 70% — because engagement quality is poor — per-claim cost rises and settlement revenue falls simultaneously. This is the most operationally sensitive assumption in the model. It is not an architecture failure, but it must be validated in the pilot before committing to at-scale staffing.

Pass — with note on CP3 execution risk.

Section 3 — T: Theorem-driven design
Pass

R1 Workaround — Schelling (1960)

Claims pooling creates a credible litigation threat — the defendant knows that not settling triggers a coordinated legal process. Schelling's focal point theory grounds the mechanism in game theory. The analogy is robust: repeated iteration of credible threats is well-documented in dispute resolution contexts. Pass.

R2 Efficacy — Spence (1973)

The pre-trial bundle disrupts defendant motivated reasoning by raising the cost of contesting the report's validity. Spence's signalling theory is one of the most robust findings in economics — Nobel-winning, extensively replicated in markets with information asymmetry. Applied here to the quality dimension of the settlement decision (whether defendants contest). Pass.

R3 Scaling / C3 — Spence (1973, recovery timing dimension)

The independent assessment org eliminates the defendant's credibility objection to the assessment report, shortening recovery time within mediation. Same theory as R2 but applied to the timing dimension (when defendants settle, not whether). The class of problem is recovery timing (maturity mismatch), not source credibility per se. Both R2 and C3 use Spence, but for different mechanistic levers. Spence (1973) is in a robust field; signalling theory has an extensive post-1973 application record including in legal contexts. Pass.

Section 5 — Conditions before capital commitment
  1. Condition 1 Reconcile fin-sim v3 for v2 architecture. The fin-sim still uses the law clinic module model (solicitor + students). It must be rebuilt to use the AOM v2 LMU specialist cost model before any financial decision rests on it. Expected impact: cost floor falls from ~£307 to ~£236, FMOS improves to ~64%. The direction is favourable — this is not a blocker for F2 design, but it is required before Phase II capital is committed.
  2. Condition 2 Validate NTP placement fee. Currently Tier 4 (£25–50/claim). At £50/claim, it adds £12.50/claim to cost floor vs the central estimate. Validate through NTP partnership scoping during R7.
  3. Condition 3 Validate LMU staff cost model. Labour is the largest RC driver (£107.67/claim). The blended rate assumption (intake £25k, EV specialist £30k, defendant engagement £35k fully loaded) must be validated through an HR benchmark exercise before at-scale hiring. Sensitivity: +20% staff costs adds ~£21.53/claim to the cost floor.
  4. Condition 4 Design customer acquisition cost in R4–R7. CAC is excluded from this gate. Adding a £50/claim CAC (Tier 4 analogue) would move FMOS from 63.6% to 56% — still passing, but reducing headroom. The final 60% gate calculation requires CAC.
  5. Condition 5 Validate pre-screen pass rate (65%). This determines how many inquiries are processed per confirmed claim. If pass rate is 45% (lower quality referrals), intake assessor cost per confirmed claim rises from £22 to £32. Sensitive to NTP referral quality.
